Object-oriented Information Systems

This page provides an overview of my progress throughout this module. This includes my weekly reflections, and relevant artefacts for each unit.

Module Learning Outcomes

  • Appraise and evaluate critically the concepts and principles of information systems.
  • Design or modify and document an object-oriented information system using appropriate tools.
  • Develop an object-oriented information system design, implementing this knowledge in applicable programming languages, such as Python and SQL.
  • Develop, implement and evaluate critically information system solutions to facilitate business decisions.

Unit 1: Introduction to Information Systems

Understanding the core elements of an information system, such as hardware, software, data, and people, helped me to appreciate the complexity and interdependence of these components. Appreciating the common issues that can arise when deploying a system, such as lack of proper project management, user-centred design, and testing, helped me to understand the importance of thorough planning and execution in the development process. Gaining an understanding of the Systems Development Lifecycle and its phases helped me to appreciate the importance of a structured approach to the development process and the need for regular review and maintenance to ensure the system remains reliable and efficient.

  • Collaborative Discussion 1: Information System Failure - Initial Post

Unit 2: Information Systems and their Importance

This unit helped me to comprehend the intricacies of an information system and the significance of considering all potential issues to ensure its success. Assessing the likelihood of any problems that an information system could face, enabled me to sharpen my critical thinking abilities and learn to pinpoint issues. It also helped me to comprehend the need for thorough planning and implementation during the development process. Additionally, it helped me understand the cruciality of regular maintenance and support for maintaining the system's reliability and efficiency.

Artefacts

  • Collaborative Discussion 1: Information System Failure - Peer Responses

Unit 3: Fundamentals of Object-Oriented Design

Identifying the appropriate objects within an information system helped me to understand the importance of breaking down complex systems into smaller, manageable components. Developing an object-oriented design for an information system helped me to understand how to efficiently organise and structure the objects within a system. Applying composition and inheritance where appropriate helped me to understand the importance of reusing code and keeping the design of the system as modular as possible.

Artefacts

  • Collaborative Discussion 1: Information System Failure - Summary
  • Practical Activity: Creating an object-oriented design

Unit 4: Object-Oriented Development and Python

By experimenting with the creation of object-oriented software using the Python language, I was able to grasp the principles of the object-oriented programming paradigm and how it can be used to build efficient and maintainable systems. This hands-on experience allowed me to develop my skills in applying concepts such as encapsulation and inheritance and in utilizing Python to create well-structured, easy to maintain code.

Artefacts

  • Object-oriented Information Systems - Codio Exercises

Unit 5: Understanding UML

This unit helped me to understand the principles of object-oriented design and how to effectively use UML to model and design systems. Using UML to develop an object-oriented system design helped me to understand how to effectively model and organize complex systems. Developing a sequence diagram to model the interactions between objects helped me to understand how to visualize the flow of interactions within a system and identify potential issues. Identifying and using the correct elements of UML to design a system helped me to understand the importance of using a standardised notation and the benefits it can bring to the design process.

Artefacts

  • Object-oriented Information Systems - Codio Exercises

Unit 6: Hands-on with UML

This unit helped me to understand how to use UML to effectively model and design systems, and to communicate the design to others. Identifying the potential classes in a system brief helped me to understand how to break down complex systems into smaller, manageable components. This helped me to understand the principles of object-oriented design and how to effectively identify and organize the classes within a system. Developing a set of UML documentation for a specific scenario helped me to understand how to use UML notation to effectively document and communicate the design of a system.

Artefacts

  • Seminar 3: Working with UML

Unit 7: Database Design

This unit helped me to understand how to design and implement efficient and maintainable databases using database design principles. I learned how to organize the data in a structured way and how to make sure that the data is consistent and accurate. Designing a database that has been correctly normalised helped me to understand the importance of eliminating data redundancy and data dependency.

Artefacts

  • Normalisation in practice
  • Mid-Module Assignment: System Design

Unit 8: Hands-on with Database Design

By developing a structure for a database for a given scenario, I was able to learn how to organize the data and make sure that the data is consistent and accurate. This helped me to understand the importance of database design and the impact it has on the performance of a system. Furthermore, creating an entity relationship diagram to document my design helped me to understand how to use a visual representation of the data to communicate and improve the design. This experience helped me to understand the process of creating a database from scratch and how to use ERD to improve it.

Artefacts

  • Collaborative Discussion 2: Alternatives to SQL - Initial Post

Unit 9: Implementing Database with SQL

Implementing a database design using SQL helped me to understand the process of creating a database structure and adding data to it. This experience helped me to understand how to use SQL to create tables, add data, and modify the structure of a database. Additionally, I was able to learn how to create appropriate queries using SQL to manage the data. Understanding the access and security issues around SQL helped me to understand the importance of securing a database and the different security measures that can be used to protect the data.

Artefacts

  • Collaborative Discussion 2: Alternatives to SQL - Peer Responses

Unit 10: Working with SQL

Through implementing a database design using SQL, I was able to gain more hands-on experience in creating and manipulating a database structure. Additionally, I was able to develop my skills in creating appropriate queries using SQL to retrieve data from the database, which helped me to understand how to use SQL to extract meaningful information from a database. Understanding the access and security issues around SQL also helped me to appreciate the importance of securing a database and the different methods that can be used to protect the data from unauthorized access.

Artefacts

  • Collaborative Discussion 2: Alternatives to SQL - Summary

Unit 11: Web Development in Python

The process of building a web application using Flask, Jinja, and SQL allowed me to gain hands-on experience in web development. This includes creating dynamic HTML templates, connecting to a database, and implementing security measures. This experience helped me understand the importance of considering all aspects of web development, including the user interface, data management, and security. Additionally, I gained a deeper understanding of the complexities involved in building a web application and the importance of testing and debugging throughout the development process. Overall, this unit has been invaluable in my growth as a software developer and has provided me with valuable skills that I can apply to future projects.

Artefacts

  • System Implementation Project: Medical Practice Management System

Unit 12: The Future of Information Systems

This unit helped me to understand the complex and ever-evolving nature of privacy in information systems. Particularly, with the rise of new technologies such as AI and IoT. By engaging with future trends in information systems, I was able to appreciate the importance of staying informed and aware of the implications of these technologies on privacy, and the importance of considering privacy as a fundamental aspect in the design and development of information systems. Additionally, this unit helped me to understand the importance of being proactive in addressing privacy concerns and ensuring that the systems we build are designed with the protection of user data in mind.
