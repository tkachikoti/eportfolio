Seminar 3: Working with UML

A class diagram is a visual representation of the classes and their relationships within an object-oriented information system. It is used to design and document the system's structure and organization.

A sequence diagram is a type of interaction diagram that shows the interactions between objects or components in a specific scenario or use case, such as booking an appointment. It is used to design and document the system's behavior and interactions.

An activity diagram is a type of flowchart that shows the flow of activities or actions within a process, such as a receptionist booking an appointment. It is used to design and document the system's workflow and business logic.

Module learning outcomes

All of these tasks are related to the module learning outcome of designing, modifying and documenting an object-oriented information system using appropriate tools, as they are all used to design and document different aspects of the system and are typically used in the development process of object-oriented information systems.
