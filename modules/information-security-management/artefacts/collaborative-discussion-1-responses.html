Collaborative Discussion 1: The Risks of Digitalisation - Peer Responses

Peer Response 1

Original post: https://www.my-course.co.uk/mod/forum/discuss.php?d=143905

Response: Great work on this post Etkin! Your reflections provide a good summary of the main points of the article by Kovaitė and Stankevičienė (2019). Your examples are well-chosen and provide a clear outline of the risks associated with Industry 4.0. Your reflections also include relevant citations to support the points made. With regard to the degree of similarity between the articles by Snieska et al. (2020) and Kovaitė and Stankevičienė (2019), the two articles approach the topic of digitalisation and its risks from different perspectives. Snieska et al. (2020) focus on technical information and innovation risks of Industry 4.0 in small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in Slovakia and Poland, while Kovaitė and Stankevičienė (2019) discuss the risks of digitalization of business models in general.

Snieska et al. (2020) emphasise the risks associated with the implementation of Industry 4.0 technologies, such as cybersecurity threats, compatibility issues, and costs, especially for SMEs. The authors highlight the importance of having adequate technical knowledge and expertise to manage these risks and avoid negative consequences. On the other hand, Kovaitė and Stankevičienė (2019) emphasise the risks associated with digital transformation in general, such as increased competition, changes in consumer behaviour, and the need for new business models.

While the two articles may seem to approach different aspects of digitalization, they are complementary in nature, as they both acknowledge the risks that come with the adoption of new technologies (Kovaitė and Stankevičienė, 2019; Snieska et al., 2020). Both articles emphasize the need for companies to understand the potential risks and develop strategies to manage them effectively (Kovaitė and Stankevičienė, 2019; Snieska et al., 2020). Snieska et al. (2020) highlight the importance of technical expertise, while Kovaitė and Stankevičienė (2019) emphasise the need for businesses to be flexible and adaptable to change. In summary, these two articles support each other by emphasising the importance of understanding the risks of digitalisation and the need for appropriate risk management strategies (Kovaitė and Stankevičienė, 2019; Snieska et al., 2020).

References:

Kovaitė, K., & Stankevičienė, J. (2019). Risks of digitalisation of business models. In Proceedings of 6th International Scientific Conference Contemporary Issues in Business, Management and Economics Engineering ‘2019.

Snieska, Vytautas & Navickas, Valentinas & Haviernikova, Katarina & Okręglicka, Małgorzata & Gajda, Waldemar. (2020). Technical, information and innovation risks of industry 4.0 in small and medium-sized enterprises – Case of Slovakia and Poland. Journal of Business Economics and Management. 21. 1269-1284.
