Information Security Management

This page provides an overview of my progress throughout this module. This includes my weekly reflections, and relevant artefacts for each unit.

Module Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and analyse security risks, threats and vulnerabilities in information systems and determine appropriate methodologies, tools, and techniques to manage and/or solve them.
  • Gather and synthesise information from multiple sources (including internet security alerts & warning sites) to aid in the systematic analysis of security breaches & issues.
  • Critically appraise and utilise methodologies, tools and techniques that help manage and audit security issues.
  • Articulate the legal, social, ethical, and professional issues faced by information security professionals.

Unit 1: An Introduction to Security and Risk Management

One of the key ways in which this unit has helped me to develop is by improving my critical thinking and analytical skills. Through reading and discussing of risks around applying digitalisation to more traditional business processes, I was able to gain a deeper understanding of the complexity of risk and the various ways in which it can be conceptualised. This helped me to develop a more nuanced approach to assessing and managing risks, taking into account multiple perspectives and factors.

Also, by exploring different approaches to quantify and qualify risks, I was able to expand my toolkit for risk assessment and management, enabling me to select the most appropriate methods for a given situation. This required me to be able to analyse and compare different approaches, weighing their advantages and disadvantages in light of specific risk scenarios.

Overall, these tasks have helped me to develop valuable skills and knowledge that will serve me well both personally and professionally. They have enabled me to approach risk management in a more nuanced and effective way. While also deepening my understanding of the broader context of information security management.

Unit 2: UML Modelling to Support Secure System Planning

In this unit, I gained knowledge on the Scrum Agile life cycle approach to project management, specifically focusing on its software development stages and the importance of prioritizing security in each stage. Through the research of academic literature and ISO/IEC standards on security, I was able to familiarize myself with the language and concepts of secure software development.

By creating a 2-column multi-line table that broke down the software development stages, I was able to apply my newfound knowledge and represent the steps involved in a process as a flow chart. This task not only helped me understand the material, but also improved my ability to organize information and present it in a clear and concise manner.

In addition, writing a blog post on how people can be managed to overcome cyber security attacks from the inside allowed me to apply my understanding of software development and security to a real-world situation. This task not only enhanced my written communication skills but also showed me the importance of considering security as a key factor in all stages of software development, not just at the end of the process.

Overall, these tasks have positively impacted my personal and professional development by providing me with a deeper understanding of secure software development and enhancing my critical thinking and communication skills. I am now better equipped to address security concerns in my future software development projects and to communicate these concerns effectively to others.

Unit 3: Programming Languages: History, Concepts & Design

The completion of this unit had a significant impact on my personal and professional development. Firstly, it helped me to gain a deeper understanding of the development of programming languages and the key milestones that have defined their evolution over time. This knowledge has increased my appreciation for the history of programming languages and how they have been shaped by the changing needs of computer systems and the users who interact with them.

Secondly, the tasks helped me to understand the key paradigms that define the different types of programming languages and how they differ in terms of syntax, functionality, and design. This knowledge has improved my ability to evaluate different programming languages and choose the most appropriate one for a particular task or project.

Thirdly, I gained a better understanding of the key concepts that determine the operation of programming languages, including data structures, algorithms, and control structures. This knowledge has increased my ability to design and implement effective and efficient software solutions.

Additionally, I learned about the key programming challenges and recommended best practices. This knowledge has helped me to understand how to approach complex problems and design software solutions that are robust and secure.

Finally, I gained an understanding of what design patterns are and when to use them. This knowledge has improved my ability to design software that is modular, maintainable, and reusable. It has also improved my ability to design software solutions that can be easily adapted to changing requirements.

I learned that linters are an essential tool for ensuring that code is error-free, consistent in its design, and of high quality.

Additionally, I compared and contrasted the outputs from linters to recognize their relevance and applicability in different development and testing scenarios. I also developed Python code that was free of error, consistent in its design, and considered to be of high quality according to common Python standards. This allowed me to apply the knowledge I gained from using linters and helped me to develop my coding skills. The ability to write high-quality code will be invaluable in my future work as a software developer, as it will help me to create robust and reliable software solutions.

Unit 7: Introduction to Operating Systems

In this unit, I read about the various mitigations that can be used to make operating systems more secure. This helped me to understand the importance of security in the field of computer science and how it affects the daily use of technology. I also read about different methods for mitigating security risks, such as implementing firewalls, encrypting data, and limiting access to sensitive information.

Additionally, I learned about some of the key risks and vulnerabilities associated with operating systems. This helped me to understand the different types of attacks that can occur and the potential harm they can cause. It also allowed me to develop my critical thinking skills as I analyzed the risks and vulnerabilities and learned to evaluate the potential impact of each.

Finally, I learned about the differences between static and dynamic libraries and when to use each. This helped me to understand the concept of libraries and how they are used in software development. I learned about the advantages and disadvantages of each type of library and how to make the right choice depending on the situation.

I was able to understand the steps involved in implementing cryptography in a software application and the importance of considering security from the start of the development process.

Overall, these tasks have had a significant impact on my personal and professional development. I now have a deeper understanding of cryptography and its applications in software development. I have learned how to choose and use cryptographic libraries effectively and how to consider security in the development process.
